Tips for Travelling from Bellarine Coastal Walk, Victoria to Melbourne or Avalon Airport

Planning ahead is key to reducing travel stress—especially when you’re departing from Bellarine Coastal Walk, Victoria, perfectly situated between two major airports: Avalon and Tullamarine. Whether it’s a quick getaway, family holiday, or business trip, these practical tips will help you enjoy a smoother, more relaxed airport transfer:

  • Book Early
    Avalon Airport is smaller and quieter than Tullamarine but can get busy during peak times like weekends, school holidays, and special events. Secure your preferred transfer time by booking in advance to avoid last-minute stress and ensure a relaxed start to your journey.

  • Double-Check Your Terminal
    Avalon has a single terminal, making navigation simple. Tullamarine, however, has multiple terminals and can be busy. Knowing your departure terminal in advance will save time and help you avoid confusion or delays at the airport.

  • Allow Plenty of Buffer Time
    Although Avalon typically has shorter queues, it’s wise to arrive early. We recommend at least 2 hours before domestic flights and 3 hours for international departures. This buffer reduces stress and leaves you time to relax before boarding.

  • Notify Us About Special Luggage or Requirements
    Traveling with extra bags, sports gear, or prams? Let us know when booking so we can dispatch the right vehicle with ample space. Whether it’s golf clubs, skis, or additional luggage, we’ll ensure a comfortable, hassle-free ride.

  • Consider Off-Peak Travel
    If your schedule allows, plan your trip during off-peak hours like early mornings or late evenings. These times usually have lighter traffic and shorter security lines, especially helpful when traveling to Avalon where access routes can be limited during busy periods.

  • Stay Updated on Traffic and Weather
    Traffic and weather conditions can impact your travel time. Check live reports before your trip to anticipate any delays and adjust your plans accordingly.
